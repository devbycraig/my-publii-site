"There Is Always a Reason"

Shifting Your Parenting Mindset

Ever wondered why your toddler suddenly hits or bites?

Or refuses to sleep? 

Or throws a tantrum?

Or won’t share?

Or fights with a sibling...

...and just when you could really do with a break! 😇

After 30 years as an early childhood teacher—working with thousands of under-5s—I can assure you: they’re not trying to make your life difficult. They’re sending you messages—clues from their inner world—and they’re doing it the only way they know how. They’re trying to get you to understand something they don’t yet have the words or the emotional tools to explain.

And if you shift from reacting to what your child is doing to asking, “What are they trying to tell me?”, your parenting will change—and you'll begin to truly understand your child's needs.

When you see your child’s behaviors as signals—not misbehavior—you’re well on the way to gentle, calm solutions for them, and more peaceful, enjoyable, and rewarding early childhood years for you (and them!).

I’m Maria Vernon—a qualified early childhood teacher, mother of four, and now a grandmother. In my new book, I reveal the reasons behind preschooler behaviours (and there’s always a reason), and I share the positive, nurturing, and respectful strategies that have worked for me—and still do.

More About My Book

Inside you'll discover how to:

  • Look for the “why” behind your child’s behavior so you can respond with empathy and insight—from big feelings like anger and defiance to quirky moments like swearing, body curiosity, or their endless “Why?” questions.

  • Apply gentle, proven strategies to handle everyday challenges like fussy eating, bedtime battles, boredom, potty learning, and emotional daycare drop-offs—bringing much more calm and confidence to your parenting.

  • Feel more grounded and connected in your parenting approach as you build a calmer and respectful relationship with your child based on understanding and guidance, not control.

  • Turn everyday routines and play into rich opportunities for learning, bonding, and celebrating your child’s unique personality and growth.

This isn’t a parenting "textbook"—it’s a practical, go-to guide for all those baffling moments that have you asking yourself, “What just happened?” or “Was that normal?” With 62 chapters arranged alphabetically in a concise, jargon-free format, you can dip in and out whenever you need answers.

Who Is The Book For?

Parents, caregivers, and educators of toddlers and preschoolers (ages 1–5) who want less frustration and more connection in their daily interactions.

When you start looking for the “why,” everything shifts. You become empowered. You’re no longer just reacting to the moment—you’re responding with calm and clarity. I believe that understanding the “why” connects you with your child and let's you guide them with empathy and confidence.

Discover the “why” behind their behavior, transform your parenting, and build a deeper connection—one understanding moment at a time.
