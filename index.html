Preschool Teachers Face a Unique Challenge

I’m not a theory-driven academic; my approach has always been practical, down-to-earth and focused on what actually works. It’s had to be. Early childhood teachers have to manage children with very diverse personalities, home environments, and levels of development. That's our job. And we have to do it fairly, we have to do it consistently, and most of all, we have to do it effectively or we’ll have chaos (and unhappy parents!).

And here’s the kicker.

Teachers must manage this assorted collection of children:

  • without conflicting with parents’ parenting styles,
  • without many of the strategies commonly used at home—like raised voices or withholding treats,
  • without punishment (it’s never allowed in an early childhood centre), and
  • without violating the rules and regulations of their center and regulatory authorities.

Early childhood teachers must find, and consistently apply, GENTLE child-management strategies that work. They simply have to.

And that’s my background.

It’s what I’ve done for all those years.

And now I’m putting all that experience into my books.
